Understanding Microservices Architecture

Microservices architecture is a style of designing and developing software systems as a collection of small, independent services that communicate over a network. This architecture style has gained popularity in recent years due to its scalability, flexibility, and ability to handle complex, distributed systems.

Key Characteristics of Microservices

  • Independence: Each service can be developed, deployed, and maintained independently.
  • Decentralization: Microservices encourage decentralized data management, where each service can manage its own database.
  • Resilience: Failures in one microservice do not impact the entire system, improving overall system reliability.
